Research projects

Five lines of work in precision motion and machining-process control. Each was developed and validated on real machine-tool hardware; the result on every card is an experimentally measured outcome.

Tool eccentricity compensation in milling

On-machine compensation that injects feed-drive micro-motions, tuned online from force/vibration feedback, with no added hardware. Converges in 2-3 spindle iterations.

ASME JMSEMM SciencePatent pending
Result86%lower peak cutting-force variation

Cross-coupled inertial vibration compensation

Trajectory pre-filtering that cancels cross-axis coupling on a 3-axis machining center, driving residual Z motion from 6 um down to about 1.2 um.

ICPE 2024Young Research Award
Result80%lower axis-coupled vibration (~1 um residual)

Active inertial damper control for precision machine tools

Learning-based feedforward control of an active inertial damper that suppresses transient vibration within actuator stroke and force limits, taking error from 12 um to 1.6 um.

CIRP AnnalsDow Scholarship
Result87%lower peak positioning error

Spindle speed control for vibration mitigation in milling

Model-free optimal spindle-speed controller driven by real-time vibration feedback, with no process model or stability-lobe map. RMS vibration cut from 72 to 9 m/s2.

CIRP JMSTASME MSEC
Result90%lower vibration vs. conventional

Chatter detection and monitoring in milling

Lightweight time-domain and PCA-based algorithms that flag chatter early at low computational cost, the sensing layer behind the closed-loop spindle controller.

Manufacturing LettersNAMRC50
ResultReal-timelow-cost early detection
