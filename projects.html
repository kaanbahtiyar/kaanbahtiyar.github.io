Research projects

Five lines of work in precision motion and machining-process control. Each was developed and validated on real machine-tool hardware; the result on every card is an experimentally measured outcome.

Active runout control

Adaptive feed-drive control that adds corrective motions on top of the nominal toolpath to compensate for tool runout so each tooth cuts evenly. The correction is tuned online using force/vibration feedback during milling. Peak cutting-force variation reduced from 101 N to 14 N in steel-alloy milling.

ASME JMSEMM SciencePatent pending
Result86%lower peak cutting-force variation

Cross-axis vibration control

Shapes the commanded motion to cancel cross-axis coupling dynamics so moving one axis no longer triggers other axis dynamics on a 3-axis machine tool. The shaping filter is fine tuned through machine-in-the-loop iterations using measured vibration responses. Peak undesired Z-axis motion during Y-axis moves fell from 6 µm to 1.2 µm.

ICPE 2024Young Research Award
Result80%lower cross-axis vibration

Active inertial damper control

Feedforward control of an active inertial damper to suppress transient vibration under actuator force and stroke constraints. The control law is iteratively refined through hardware-in-the-loop experiments. Peak positioning error reduced from 12 µm to 1.6 µm.

CIRP AnnalsDow Scholarship
Result87%lower peak positioning error

Spindle speed control for chatter mitigation

Model-free adaptive spindle speed control that eliminates chatter and minimizes total vibration from real-time vibration feedback, deployed through the CNC’s standard spindle-override input. RMS vibration cut from 131 to 20 m/s² in milling tests.

CIRP JMSTASME MSEC
Result85%lower RMS vibration

Real-time chatter detection

Computationally efficient, real-time chatter detection using time-domain and statistical features. The algorithms identify chatter onset and serve as the trigger input for the closed-loop spindle speed controller.
