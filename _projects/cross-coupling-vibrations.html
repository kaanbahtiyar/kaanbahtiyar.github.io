Cross-axis vibration control

80%↓
Axis-coupled Z tool-tip vibration reduced from 6 µm to about 1.2 µm (5× lower) on a vertical machining center, while enabling more aggressive axis acceleration for higher throughput.

Overview

On a C-frame vertical machining center, a hard acceleration of the table in Y does not stay in Y: the inertial reaction rocks the column, and the rocking mode shows up as Z-direction vibration at the tool tip — even though the Z axis never received a command. The same coupling exists between X and Z. This cross-axis vibration degrades surface finish during cutting moves and forces conservative acceleration limits on non-cutting moves, which directly caps cycle time and throughput.

Approach

A cross-talk dynamics compensator (CTDC) generates a small, synchronized Z-axis command from the commanded X/Y motion, shaped so that the commanded Z motion and the structurally induced Z motion cancel at the tool tip.

Design is two-stage. A frequency-domain fit around the excited rocking mode gives an initial compensator; a machine-in-the-loop iterative learning pass then refines it from measured vibration responses, so final performance does not depend on a perfect frequency-response model. Because the compensator acts as a trajectory-level pre-filter, it drops in ahead of the existing servo loops and CNC execution without touching them.

What is novel

  • Cancels cross-axis coupling explicitly, rather than only shaping the commanded axis.
  • Combines a simple identification with machine-in-the-loop learning, making it robust to model error.
  • Lets the machine run shorter acceleration time constants — more throughput — without sacrificing finish.

Results

On a 3-axis vertical machining center, peak coupled Z-direction tool-tip error during Y-axis moves fell from 6 µm to about 1.2 µm — an 80% (5×) reduction — while permitting more aggressive axis acceleration. The work received the Young Research Award at ICPE 2024 (JSPE, Sendai, Japan).
