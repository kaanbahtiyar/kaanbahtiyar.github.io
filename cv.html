Experience

Graduate Research Assistant, Oregon State University2020-Present
  • Developed a learning-based feedforward strategy for an actuated inertial damper, reducing peak vibration 87% on a feed-drive testbed under actuator stroke/force constraints.
  • Characterized cross-axis coupling on a 3-axis machine tool and built trajectory pre-filtering that cut axis-coupled vibration 80%, into the sub-micron (about 1 um) range.
  • Developed data-driven chatter detection and an optimal real-time vibration-feedback spindle-speed controller, eliminating chatter and cutting forced vibration up to 90% versus conventional control.
  • Developed an adaptive tool-eccentricity compensation method via feed modulation, cutting peak cutting-force variation 86% and supporting a pending patent.
  • Trained and mentored graduate and undergraduate students; guided two M.Sc. theses.
