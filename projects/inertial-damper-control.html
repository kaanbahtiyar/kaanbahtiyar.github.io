Active inertial damper control for precision machine tools

87%↓
Peak positioning error cut from 12 um to 1.6 um in about 10 learning iterations, within tighter actuator stroke and force limits than conventional damping.
Hardware & instrumentation
Feed-drive testbedVoice-coil & linear-motor dampersLinear/rotary encodersDisplacement sensorMATLAB/Simulink RTI

Overview

Fast point-to-point motion excites structural modes of machine tools and robots, producing transient and residual vibration and overshoot that limit how aggressively you can move while holding precision. Feedback-only damping cannot fully remove the initial overshoot.

Approach

The damper applies a task-specific, pre-scheduled feedforward signal, learned from measured vibration over repeated motions, on top of its feedback controller.

The feedforward is parameterized with filtered B-spline basis functions: few control points, smooth signals, fast optimization, with actuator stroke and force constraints enforced during learning.

It needs no tight integration with the host NC, which keeps it practical to deploy.

What is novel

  • Pre-scheduled, task-specific feedforward eliminates overshoot a feedback-only damper leaves behind.
  • Low-dimensional B-spline parameterization makes tuning fast and repeatable.
  • Constraint-aware optimization enables more compact damper hardware.

Results

The learned feedforward converges in about 10 iterations and reduces peak positioning error by 87% (12 um to 1.6 um) while respecting force and stroke limits, reaching the plus/minus 2 um band immediately instead of waiting out a settle.
