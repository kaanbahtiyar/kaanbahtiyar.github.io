Cross-coupled inertial vibration compensation

80%↓
Axis-coupled Z vibration reduced from 6 um to about 1.2 um (5x lower) on a vertical machining center, while enabling more aggressive axis acceleration for higher throughput.
Hardware & instrumentation
3-axis vertical machining centerAccelerometersLaser encoderdSPACE real-time I/OMATLAB/Simulink RTI

Overview

During high-acceleration feed moves, inertial loads excite structural modes and create axis-coupled vibration: motion in one axis (say Y) induces tool-tip vibration in another (Z). Even in non-cutting moves this degrades surface finish and limits how hard you can accelerate the axes, which directly caps productivity.

Approach

A cross-talk dynamics compensator (CTDC) generates a synchronized Z-axis command from the commanded X/Y motion, shaping it so the net Z tool-tip motion cancels the coupling.

Design is two-stage: a frequency-domain fit gives an initial compensator around the excited resonance, then an iterative learning pass refines it from measured data so performance does not depend on a perfect FRF.

It works as a trajectory-level pre-filter, so it drops in alongside the existing servo loops and CNC execution.

What is novel

  • Cancels cross-axis coupling explicitly rather than just shaping a single axis.
  • Combines simple identification with machine-in-the-loop tuning for robustness to model error.
  • Lets the machine run more aggressive acceleration without sacrificing finish.

Results

Peak coupled Z-direction error fell from 6 um to 1.2 um, about 80% (5x lower) under the tested Y-axis motion, while allowing shorter acceleration time constants for greater throughput. The work received the ICPE 2024 Young Research Award.
