Spindle speed control for chatter mitigation

90%↓
Up to 90% lower vibration than conventional chatter-focused control (RMS 72 to 9 m/s2), using a model-free controller driven by real-time vibration feedback.
Hardware & instrumentation
3-axis CNC machine toolAccelerometersSpindle override via PLCdSPACE real-time I/OMATLAB/Simulink RTI

Overview

Milling vibration comes as self-excited chatter (unstable) and periodic forced vibration (stable). Both wreck surface finish, shorten tool life, and can damage the machine. Model-based planning (stability lobes, SLE maps) needs heavy identification and does not transfer across machines; chatter-only speed control can suppress chatter but amplify forced vibration near resonances.

Approach

Spindle-speed regulation is posed as an online optimization: compute a vibration cost from accelerometer data and update speed to reduce it.

An extremum-seeking control framework estimates the cost gradient from measured data, with no process model. A dither-guided variant injects a small speed perturbation; a dither-free variant fits the gradient from buffered speed and cost data via least squares.

It runs in real time, applying speed updates through the machine's spindle-override input and PLC.

What is novel

  • Optimizes for total vibration (forced plus chatter), avoiding the usual chatter-versus-forced tradeoff.
  • Fully model-free and on-machine, with no stability-lobe identification.
  • Data-driven gradient estimation makes it deployable without modal testing.
Real-time spindle-speed adaptation during a milling test: the controller activates after chatter develops and drives the vibration down.

Results

The controller updates speed online from acceleration feedback and cuts vibration 87% (RMS 72 to 9 m/s2), converging to a speed with about 90% lower vibration than a conventional chatter-focused approach that parks speed near resonance.
